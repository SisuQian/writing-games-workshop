简介Introduction

乌力波（法语：Ouvroir de littérature potentielle, Oulipo）意为“潜在文学工场”，于1960年在巴黎创立，旨在通过人为限制探查语言的潜在结构。 它将文学从灵感中心转向可设计、可生成的模式实验。20世纪60—70年代，随着乔治·佩雷克等成员的加入，乌力波系统化发展出多种写作方法，并与结构主义的语言观形成共振，强调文本由结构生成而非单纯表达。 1990年代起，乌力波在数字人文与算法写作领域获得新解释，被视为生成式文本与程序性写作的早期模型，其影响跨越文学、数学、游戏研究与概念艺术。

Oulipo (Ouvroir de littérature potentielle), founded in Paris in 1960, explores the latent structures of language through deliberately designed constraints. It shifts writing away from inspiration-as-origin toward reproducible, generative experiments. With members such as Georges Perec, Oulipo developed a systematic repertoire of methods resonating with structuralist views of text as produced by form. Since the 1990s, it has been re-read through digital humanities and algorithmic writing—as an early model for procedural and generative text across literature, mathematics, game studies, and conceptual art.

理念Concept

写作不仅是表达工具，也是可以被设计、被触发、被生成的实践。 本工作坊把“限制”当作通道：让结构先行，让语言在规则中自行生长。

关键词：规则 / 生成 / 结构 / 共同阅读与互评

Writing is not only expression but a practice that can be designed, triggered, and generated. Here, constraints function as a passage: structure leads, and language grows inside rules—through collective reading and iterative feedback.

方法Method

在「SISU乌力波写作游戏工作坊」中，乌力波作为即兴创作的通道，以游戏介入写作， 在跨越方法与多语境下体验乌力波的创作乐趣。愿每位参与者沉浸其中，有所收获。

形式：短规则写作 + 观察/记录 + 讨论与改写（可按场地定制）

Within the SISU Oulipo Writing Games Workshop, Oulipo works as a channel for improvisation. We approach writing through play, testing constraints across contexts and methods. Participants move between exercises, observation/notation, discussion, and rewriting—customizable to the venue and audience.
